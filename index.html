The Fog of Potential

In Brazil, childhood is often a negotiation with scarcity.
Education is not a promise—it’s a gamble.
Health is not a right—it’s a lottery.
And the future? It’s a fog that only a few can see through.

When I look at the numbers, I don’t just see statistics—I see stolen potential.
Only 43.6% of second graders can read proficiently.
Only 20.6% of children under 9 eat adequately each day.

In wealthier nations, thriving is the expectation.
Here, surviving is the hope.

We need dashboards not just to display numbers—but to bear witness.
The fog isn’t inevitable—it’s constructed.
And it is our job to clear it.

“A child without learning is not just forgotten—they are erased.”
